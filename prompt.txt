# Professional App Changes

## 1. Bidding Screen State Management

Replace the current bottom-up job animation.

The screen should have three states:

### Offline

Display an animated message:

> **You are not online.**

---

### Online but No Jobs

Animate the text to:

> **Searching for jobs...**

If no jobs are available:

> **There are no jobs at the moment.**

---

### Jobs Available

As soon as jobs are received:

* Remove all status text.
* Display the jobs directly in a scrollable list.
* Do not animate cards from the bottom.

---

# 2. Real-Time Jobs (WebSockets)

Use **WebSockets** to keep a persistent connection between the frontend and backend.

Requirements:

* Backend immediately pushes new jobs.
* Backend continuously sends an empty state (`null`/`0`) when no jobs exist.
* Frontend updates instantly without polling or manual refresh.

Research and implement proper WebSocket lifecycle management on the frontend (connect, reconnect, disconnect, error handling, app background/foreground).

---

# 3. Pull to Refresh

Allow pull-to-refresh on the jobs list.

When triggered:

* Perform a hard refresh.
* Display a loading spinner.
* Show **"Refreshing..."** above the first job while refreshing.
* Remove the indicator once complete.

---

# 4. Job List

Each job card should contain only minimal information.

Display **three** quick bid options:

* Task Price
* +5%
* +10%

Example:

Task Price: Rs.1000

```
[1000]   [1050 (+5%)]   [1100 (+10%)]
```

---

# 5. Job Details Bottom Sheet

When the professional taps a job:

Open a draggable Bottom Sheet that:

* Opens at half height.
* Can be dragged to full screen.
* Can be collapsed back down.

---

# 6. Bottom Sheet Content

Include:

### Quick Bid Row

Three options:

* Base Price
* +5%
* +10%

### Custom Bid

A text input for entering a custom amount.

### Bid Button

Initially displays:

> **Bid at Rs.1000**

If +5% is selected:

> **Bid at Rs.1050**

If +10% is selected:

> **Bid at Rs.1100**

All calculations should be performed on the frontend.

---

# 7. Quick Bidding from Job List

The three price buttons shown directly on the job card should also allow instant bidding.

On successful bid:

Show a toast:

> **You have successfully placed a bid of Rs.XXXX.**

---

# 8. Waiting State After Bid

Immediately after placing a bid:

Display a status bar:

> **Waiting for user to accept/decline...**

Duration:

**10 seconds (fixed).**

During these 10 seconds:

* Disable all bid buttons.
* Prevent bid modification.
* Prevent placing another bid for that job.

---

# 9. Bid Result

### If accepted

Automatically navigate the professional to the **Active Task** screen.

### If not accepted within 10 seconds

* Remove the waiting bar.
* Re-enable bid buttons.
* Allow the professional to place another bid.

---

# Customer App Changes

## 10. Live Incoming Bids

Use WebSockets so that customer receives bids instantly as professionals place them.

---

## 11. Bid Visibility

Each incoming bid should remain visible for exactly:

**10 seconds**

After 10 seconds:

* Automatically remove the bid from the customer's screen.

---

# Technical Requirements

## WebSockets

Use WebSockets for:

* Live job availability
* New jobs
* New bids
* Bid acceptance
* Bid rejection
* Active task updates

Avoid traditional polling.

---

## Pull to Refresh

Professional jobs screen should support:

* Pull-to-refresh
* Hard refresh
* Loading indicator
* "Refreshing..." label

---

## Bottom Sheets

Use a draggable bottom sheet that supports:

* Half-screen presentation
* Full-screen expansion
* Swipe gestures

This specification is now internally consistent:

* **3 quick bid options** everywhere (Base Price, +5%, +10%).
* **10-second** waiting/expiration window as the fixed final value.
